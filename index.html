The Origins of the Internet & The World Wide Web

Explore how the web reshaped the world

Origins of the Internet

The Internet originated with ARPANET in 1969, a project connecting research institutions to securely share information. It laid the foundation for the modern digital era.

The World Wide Web

In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee proposed the World Wide Web, a system to access interlinked documents using hypertext. The first website became publicly available in 1991.

The Rise of Search Engines

As websites rapidly grew during the 1990s, finding specific information became challenging. Search engines like AltaVista, Yahoo!, and later Google transformed web navigation by indexing billions of pages and ranking results by relevance. Today, they are essential tools for accessing information on the Internet.
